All documents from the 9th, 10th and 11th centuries assign the place the name of Arcis Marmoricis. Because the tomb of the Saint was found there, the Latin generic Locus Sanctus (Holy Place) and Locus Sancti Iacobi (Place of Santiago) were sometimes used. One of the interpretations, uncertain,9 is that Compostela derives from the Latin expression campus stellae, which means “field of the star”, after the star that, according to the miracle, appeared to Teodomiro indicating this place.
